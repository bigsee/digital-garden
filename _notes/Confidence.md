title: Confidence
---

Having confidence is a prerequisite for taking risks. Over-confidence can lead to distorted or inadequate thinking. 
Confidence seems to have an inverse relationship with complexity: the less complex an endeavour, the more confidence one
is likely to have about one's eventual success.

I believe that driving is statistically more likely to result in crashes and fatalities than flying. Despite this, 
drivers seem routinely to take far greater and more regular risks. Could this relationship be why?

### Working questions

1. What is the Dunning-Krueger effect and how might it relate here?
2. Exactly _how/why_ does overconfidence lead to distorted or inadequate thinking?
