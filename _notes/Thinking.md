title: Thinking
---

In today's knowledge economy, most of us are paid to think. Our professional success is directly related to our ability 
to filter and process information and then make good decisions based on it. We should devote much more time to exploring
_how_ and _why_ we think, to improve the quality of that thinking.

Most of our actions are automatic, rather than the result of a rational, logical thinking process as we often believe. 
Even when our thinking _is_ intentional, it is held hostage by our [cognitive biases](/Bias.md). The combined effects of this bias 
results in four default modes of thinking:

1. The Emotion default
2. The Social default
3. The Ego default 
4. The Inertia default

### Working questions

1. How can we avoid our thinking being limited by the defaults?
2. Which tools can we use to strengthen our thinking?
