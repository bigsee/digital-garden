Airports are not usually designed as spaces to enjoy. They are not meant to be inhabited, but negotiated.

Extraction, filtration and channeling are the priorities, rather than comfort. They are places in which to feel hurried with everything urgent. The whole rhythm of the place is on a schedule.

There is usually a feeling of being herded like cattle.

### Working questions

1. How might one perceive an airport from a psycho-sociological point of view?
2. How has the micro-economy of airports developed?
